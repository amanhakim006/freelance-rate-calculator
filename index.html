Why Freelancers Need to Calculate Their Rate?

Many new freelancers make the mistake of guessing their hourly rate based on what others are charging. This often leads to burnout. Using a Freelance Rate Calculator helps you factor in non-billable hours, taxes, and business expenses.

Understanding Billable vs. Non-Billable Hours

You might work 40 hours a week, but you can likely only bill for 25-30 hours. The rest goes into finding clients, emails, and accounting. Our tool adjusts your rate so you get paid for your entire effort, not just the design time.

The Importance of Time Off

Unlike a salaried job, freelancers don't get paid holidays. If you plan to take 4 weeks off per year, you need to earn your annual income in just 48 weeks. This calculator automatically increases your hourly rate to cover your vacation time.
